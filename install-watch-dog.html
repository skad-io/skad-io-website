Installing Watch Dog

All you need to know to get that puppy barking.

Your Watch Dog by SKAD has arrived

That's pretty exciting. I bet you're keen to get going.

Let us tell you how.

Feed your Watch Dog

Watch Dog needs to be connected to both the power and to your home network.

Watch Dog gets its power through a micro USB cable. Plug the power into the outer of the two micro USB ports.

Plug the provided micro-USB-to-ethernet adapter into the inner micro USB port – the middle of the three visible ports on the long side. Use the provided ethernet-to-ethernet cable to connect the adapter to your router.

Home Watch Dog in the DMZ

Watch Dog needs to be in the DMZ of your home network.

Putting a device in the DMZ is a task you have to complete on your router. Because of this, the method differs depending on the type of router you have.

What is the DMZ?

What is the DMZ?

DMZ stands for demilitarized zone and is the term used for the part of your home network that is accessible from the external internet.

Your router puts a firewall between the devices on your home network and the big bad web. To see all the attacks coming at your home, Watch Dog has to sit in the DMZ, on the other side of that firewall. (Don't worry. Our pups are tough; they can take it.)

Want to know more about the DMZ? Wikipedia is as good a place as any to start.

General router instructions

General router instructions

  1. Go to your router's administration pages.

    To do this, open a browser window and type the IP address of your router. This address is of the form http://192.168.x.x.

  2. You might have to find out the IP address of your Watch Dog.

    Often, connected devices are listed on the router home page. Otherwise look for a connected devices tab or menu item. The pup appears as skad-dog in the list of devices.

    If you can opt for your Watch Dog to always have the same IP address, do so. This keeps things working seamlessly if the pup or your router are rebooted.

  3. Find the DMZ settings.

    The location of these settings varies depending on the type of router you have. Look for menu items such as Settings, Advanced, Port forwarding, WAN setup, or DMZ.

  4. Select your Watch Dog from the list of devices that can be put in the DMZ or type in its IP address.

    The pup appears as skad-dog in the list of devices.

Instructions for BT Home Hub

Instructions for BT Home Hub

  1. Go to your router's administration page.

    This is usually found at http://192.168.1.254.

    On the Home page, under My Home Network, you can see skad-dog listed.

  2. Set your Watch Dog to have a fixed IP address.

    This prevents the pup from falling out of the DMZ if it or your router are rebooted.

    Select the Settings tab, then click Home Network > skad-dog. Under Addressing, click Yes to Always use this IP address.

  3. Go to the DMZ settings.

    Select the Settings tab, then click Advanced Settings > Port Forwarding > DMZ.

    For some versions of the BT Home Hub: DMZ is under Firewall instead of Port Forwarding.

  4. Enable the DMZ and put your Watch Dog in it.

    Beside DMZ Enable, click the Yes button. Select skad-dog from the dropdown list of devices, and click Apply

Instructions for Sky Hub

Instructions for Sky Hub

  1. Go to your router's administration page.

    This is usually found at http://192.168.0.1.

  2. Find out the IP address of your Watch Dog.

    Select the Maintenance tab, then click Attached Devices.

    The pup appears as skad-dog in the list of devices. Make a note of the associated IP address.

  3. Go to the DMZ settings.

    Select the Advanced tab, then click WAN Setup.

  4. Enable the DMZ and put your Watch Dog in it.

    Click the checkbox beside Default DMZ Server, type in the IP address of your Watch Dog, and click Apply

Instructions for Virgin Media SuperHub

Instructions for Virgin Media SuperHub

  1. Go to your router's administration page.

    This is usually found at http://192.168.0.1.

  2. Find out the IP address of your Watch Dog.

    Select the Device Connection Status button.

    The pup appears as skad-dog in the list of devices. Make a note of the associated IP address.

  3. Go to the DMZ settings.

    Go back to the home page and click the Advanced Settings button at the bottom of the page. In the Security table, click DMZ.

  4. Enable the DMZ and put your Watch Dog in it.

    Click the checkbox to enable the DMZ, type in the last part of the IP address of your Watch Dog, and click Apply
